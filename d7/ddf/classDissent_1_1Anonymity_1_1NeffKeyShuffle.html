Detailed Description

In Neff's Key Shuffle, each member generates key pair (g, y_i), where all members have a common generator (g), modulo, and subgroup.

Where y_i in modulou and y_i = g ^ x_i with x_i in subgroup. Members then transmit their y_i to the first server, who rebases the generator via: g_j = g_(j - 1) ^ r, where r in subgroup, where g_(-1) is the common shared g. The member also changes bases on the public keys: y_i_j = y_i_(j - 1) ^ r. Both y_j (in ascending order) and g_j are transmitted to the next server who performs the same operations. Finally, y_k and g_k are transmitted to the clients, who find their slot by calculating their public key via y_i_k = g_k ^ x_i.

Because of the nature of this round, it is very different than other protocol rounds. There is no input and there are no automated outputs. Outputs need to be explicitly taken via the objects public methods.
